# Ledger & Lore Reimbursement Policy

This is a totally fabricated policy for demo purposes. It should feel crisp, opinionated, and just plausible enough to be funny.

## Core rule

Approve ordinary business spend. Reject obviously personal nonsense. Escalate the expensive, theatrical, politically awkward, or unusually creative middle.

## Automatic approval lanes

Approve if documentation exists and none of the rejection or escalation rules apply.

- Local meals under `$90` for one person
- Team meals under `$180` total when the attendees are named
- Rideshare, taxi, train, and airfare booked for real travel
- Lodging up to `$450` per night during business travel
- Office supplies under `$120`
- Dry cleaning or emergency wardrobe fixes under `$40` if tied to an on-camera appearance, conference talk, or customer dinner
- Software, SaaS, and cloud spend that clearly maps to work

## Automatic rejection lanes

Reject when the expense is clearly personal, vanity-driven, or unserious in a way that no additional documentation could rescue.

- Alcohol billed as “founder resilience”
- Gifts for romantic partners, ex-cofounders, or pets
- Personal wellness treatments with no concrete work context
- Clothing purchases other than minor emergency repair or cleaning
- Home goods, furniture, decor, candles, incense, crystals, and “workspace aura” items for a private residence
- Parking tickets, speeding tickets, and “creative interpretation” of local law
- Crypto losses, sports betting, karaoke healing sessions, or apology flowers
- Any expense whose main justification is “vibes,” “manifestation,” “main character energy,” or “premium narrative alignment”

## Automatic escalation lanes

Escalate when the expense is weird enough that a human should own the precedent.

- Any single expense over `$250`
- Animal-related spend of any kind
- Luxury wellness, sauna, cold plunge, massage, or retreat-like spending
- Ceremonial, ritual, blessing, or “vision quest” framing
- Helicopters, horses, boats, private drivers, or unusually cinematic transportation
- Group meals over `$180`
- Lodging over `$450` per night
- Expenses tied to brand shoots, launch parties, offsites, or investor entertainment
- Anything defensible only if the employee is technically right but spiritually suspicious

## Missing-doc rules

- If the expense might be valid but the receipt is missing, reject only when the policy already clearly forbids it.
- Otherwise escalate if the amount is high or the story is strange.
- Otherwise ask for more info if the system supports that state.

## Special exception rules

### The On-Camera Exception

Approve modest appearance-related fixes when they prevent visible embarrassment during a public event.

Examples:
- emergency blazer steaming
- lint roller purchase before a keynote
- same-day shoe repair before a customer presentation

### The Goat Clause

Any expense involving animals is never auto-approved, even if a user insists it was “customer empathy research.”

Default outcome: escalate.

### The Founder's Theater Rule

If the expense sounds like something a person would do while trying to have a breakthrough instead of a meeting, escalate it.

Examples:
- horseback strategy session
- sauna debrief
- sound bath offsite
- ceremonial notebook
- smoke machine for product launch ambience

### The Hard No Rule

If the employee is trying to expense identity, taste, or emotional recovery as if it were a business necessity, reject it.

## Recommended phrasing

Use language like:

- “Approve under the On-Camera Exception.”
- “Reject under the Hard No Rule.”
- “Escalate under the Founder’s Theater Rule.”
- “Escalate under the Goat Clause.”

Keep the explanation concise. One strong paragraph is better than a mini essay.
